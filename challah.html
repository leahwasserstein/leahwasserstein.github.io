Challah Recepie

BASIC CHALLAH RECIPE (makes 2):

Ingredients:
1 cup warm water
½ cup canola/vegetable oil
¼ cup honey
1 whole egg / 2 yolks (I usually use XL eggs)
¼ cup sugar plus 1 tablespoon
2 teaspoons kosher salt
4 ½ - 3/4 cup high quality bread flour (I like King Arthur)- if you dont have bread flour you can use all-purpose flour
1 Tablesoon instant yeast or 1 packet of instant yeast
Additional egg for egg wash

Directions:
In a large bowl, combine water, 1 Tbs sugar, and yeast. Wait a few minutes for it to start bubbling.
Stir in oil, honey, eggs, flour, salt and sugar.
Turn out onto a floured board and knead for 5 minutes. If sticky, add additional flour, 1 tablespoon at a time until desired consistency is reached.
Allow to rise in a large bowl in a dark place, covered with plastic wrap or a towel for 1 hour. Make sure it has room to grow. Punch down about 6 times in the bowl- do not take it out of the bowl. Cover and allow dough to rise again for 2 hours.
Once it has risen, remove the dough from the bowl. Using a dough or pizza cutter, divide into 2 equal portions. Then divide each of those into 3 portions.
On a lightly floured surface, roll three portions into long ropes, then carefully braid together, then place on a parchment covered cookie sheet. Repeat with remaining dough.
*Stuffed Challah OPTIONAL: Roll the three portions into long ropes. Flatten the ropes. Line filling along each of the strands. Close the dough completely around the filling, so they look like ropes again and braid them like usual.
Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
Cover with a towel and let rise for an additional 45 minutes in a draft-free place.
Brush loaves with egg wash (1 egg plus 2-3 teaspoons of water). Add a topping if you wish (ex. seeds, salt flakes, everything but the bagel topping)
Bake for approximately 26-28 minutes or until golden brown.
